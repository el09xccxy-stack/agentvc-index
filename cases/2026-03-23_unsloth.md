## One-Liner

Open-source LLM fine-tuning library delivering 2x speed and 70% memory reduction, with 57K stars and 150M+ downloads — the de facto community standard for local fine-tuning.

## Key Facts

- **Stars:** 57,000+ | **Downloads:** 150M+
- **Founded by:** Daniel & Michael Han (Australia-based brothers)
- **Funding:** ~$500K seed (Redpoint Ventures scout, Samsung NEXT)
- **Key Partnerships:** Google, OpenAI, Meta, NVIDIA (official fine-tuning partner)
- **Commercial Product:** Unsloth Pro (multi-GPU, enhanced speed), Unsloth Studio (desktop app)
- **License:** Apache 2.0

---

## Dimension Scores

### A. Ecosystem Health (25%) — Score: 8.5

57K GitHub stars and 150M downloads represent exceptional community traction — surpassing most funded AI infra companies. Contributor base is growing, and the project has become the default fine-tuning tool referenced in tutorials, courses, and documentation across the ecosystem. Every major open model release (Llama, Gemma, Qwen) drives incremental Unsloth adoption. Google, OpenAI, Meta, and NVIDIA have all formally acknowledged or partnered with the project.

### B. Team & Governance (20%) — Score: 7.5

Daniel and Michael Han are technically strong — Daniel has an NVIDIA background and is prolific on GitHub. However, the team is extremely small for the scale of the project. Only $500K raised to date is unusual for a project with this adoption. Governance is founder-centric; no visible advisory board or independent governance structure.

### C. Technical Moat (20%) — Score: 8.0

Kernel-level CUDA/Triton optimizations for QLoRA/LoRA fine-tuning are non-trivial to replicate. Support for 100+ LLM architectures creates significant switching costs. The 2x speed and 70% VRAM reduction claims are independently verified. However, NVIDIA's own optimization efforts and model providers internalizing fine-tuning could pressure the moat. AMD GPU portability is a secondary differentiator.

### D. Product-Market Fit (20%) — Score: 8.5

150M downloads is unarguable PMF. The fine-tuning tooling market grows structurally with every new open model release. Canva and NASA reported as users/interested parties. Unsloth Pro offers a natural upsell from the OSS funnel. The local-first narrative aligns with on-device/on-prem AI trends.

### E. Exit Potential (15%) — Score: 7.5

Natural acquisition target for NVIDIA, cloud providers, or LLM companies wanting to own the fine-tuning layer. 150M downloads create massive strategic distribution value. IPO path requires significant revenue scaling from seed stage. Redpoint and Samsung NEXT involvement suggest pathway to larger rounds. Key question: why only $500K raised — extreme capital efficiency or raising difficulty?

---

## Composite Score: 8.10 → 🟡 Yellow (Strong) + Conditions

**Conditions:** Highest score in W13 batch. Near Green threshold. Key upgrade triggers: (a) Series A at >$50M valuation, (b) Unsloth Pro revenue disclosure, (c) formal hyperscaler integrated fine-tuning partnership. Key risk: the funding gap — 57K stars and 150M downloads on $500K seed is either bootstrapping mastery or a flag requiring DD clarification.

---

## Portfolio Action

**Immediate Follow.** Request Series A participation. Clarify funding anomaly. If raising at <$100M, this is attractive. Priority DD question: Studio commercial strategy and enterprise tier pricing model.
