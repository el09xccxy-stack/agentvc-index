## One-Liner

Multi-agent financial trading framework simulating institutional trading desks with specialized AI agents — high fork ratio signals quantitative community experimentation, but pre-commercial with academic origins.

## Key Facts

- **Origin:** Academic research team (Tauric Research)
- **Fork Ratio:** ~18% (abnormally high — indicates heavy experimentation)
- **Architecture:** Multi-agent system with specialized roles (analyst, risk manager, trader, etc.)
- **Product:** Terminal (unreleased) — planned commercial interface
- **Live Performance:** Not yet validated with real capital
- **License:** Open source

## Dimension Scores

### A. Ecosystem Health (25%) — Score: 6.5

18% fork ratio is the standout metric — quantitative traders and researchers are actively experimenting. Signals genuine technical interest from high-value audience. Star count and contributor base are modest. No major integrations or downstream dependencies yet.

### B. Team & Governance (20%) — Score: 5.5

Academic team without visible track record in production financial software or company-building. No clear CTO/CEO separation or business leadership. Governance is informal.

### C. Technical Moat (20%) — Score: 7.0

Multi-agent trading desk simulation is architecturally interesting — specialized agents for different trading functions mirrors real institutional workflows. However, agent orchestration patterns are increasingly commoditized. Real moat requires live performance data showing alpha generation — currently absent.

### D. Product-Market Fit (20%) — Score: 6.0

Quantitative trading is a proven market with willingness to pay. Fork ratio suggests community sees value. Terminal product is unreleased, no revenue or enterprise adoption.

### E. Exit Potential (15%) — Score: 6.0

Potential acquisition by trading platforms (QuantConnect, Alpaca), hedge funds, or fintech companies. Without live performance data and revenue, project is pre-exit-ready.

---

## Composite Score: 6.35 → 🟠 Watch

**Watch Conditions:** Revisit when: (a) Terminal product launches, (b) live trading performance data available, (c) team adds business leadership.
